There is a certain weary patience in Mr. Gordon
Craig's attitude toward the public. Because
what he has to say is so important, so valuable, and
so profoundly true, he does not expect to
make himself understood; still less does he expect
that more than half a dozen of the elect will
listen to him. . . . . a number of fine etchings of
remarkable draughtsmanship . . . . . A good deal of
graceful sword play to begin with, which Mr. Craig
sets forth . . . the ignorance & indifference . . . . of
the public; and then presuming we choose to
be one of the six who are able to understand
and willing to listen, the writer takes us
generously & painstakingly into his confidence.
