Tappé. Christmas–the mere thought of it
. . . kindles such a glow of joviality & good fellowship
in my imagination that I find my charming wraps
and gowns, hats, and fans reflecting my
mood in surprising ways. A long evening wrap
wh I had meant to develop in sober brown &
dusky mink blossomed into being under my
very eyes as flame velvet edged w snowy fox.
. . . . . I noted w interest that a waist length
jacket of nutria held out against this impulse
towards unbridled, garish gaiety, and prolonged
itself to the ankles with matching cloth (Linnet)
although I could have wished that it might
have chosen mistletoe green or holly berry red
in place of the more reticent color. . . . Last of
