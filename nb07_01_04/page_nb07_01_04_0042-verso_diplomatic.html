Outlook 13 June 1923       "To Fellow-Fans." The Outlook 134 (13 June 1923): 163-164.
To fellow-fans. In the realm of baseball . . .
there are 4 races of inhabitants–inmates some
would call them–fans, rooters, professional guys
& back lot bugs. *
Sand on the Diamond. Gilbert Patten
But Rockland had a mixed team of all sorts, a
scrappy cock-a-hoop aggregation that captured
games any old way they could. He caught
Morse's 1st smoker on the end of his bat
& laced it over the field in centre field.
We nosed out a victory after 10 innings that
nearly made nervous wrecks of some of the
spectators.
