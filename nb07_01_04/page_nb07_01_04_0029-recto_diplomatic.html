Chance for Inventors:       MM quotes from Sir William Bell, "Chance for Inventors," in the poem and Notes to "An Octopus," first published in Observations. New York: The Dial Press, 1924, 83-90. Sir Wm Bell of the Brit. Institute
of Patentees has made list of inventions that
he says the world needs. His list includes
Glass that will bend
A smooth road surface that will not be slippery
in wet weather.
A furnace that will conserve 95% of its heat.
A process to make flannel unshrinkable
A noiseless airplane & an airplane that can be
managed safely & easily by a boy or girl.
A motor engine of one pound weight per horsepower
Methods to reduce friction.
Practical ways of utilizing the Tides
A process to extract phosphorus from
vulcanized India rubber so that it can be boiled
up & used again.
A temperance drink that will keep & yet not pall
