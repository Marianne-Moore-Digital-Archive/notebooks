(Generalizations on courtship)
They do not apply to super emotional people,
to artists, . . or to the unduly intellectual
Such monsters must look after themselves
in the lists of love. . . . Success today
is no more likely than success a century
ago, unless one applies to courtship
a certain degree of low cunning.
(must give) to stony places a light
dressing of good earth.
Intelligently used poverty can be as
affective as wealth. . . Most women
are highly responsive to the attraction of
the arts, of letters, of music. They do
not understand them better than men
but they like them better. If he has no
wings . . . let him reserve for matrimony
the masc. privileges of crudity--& ignorance
¶ Assuming that he is reasonably young,
not too ill looking, not too poor, it may
almost be said that any man can wed
any woman . . . . She may be sorry
later that she has done so.
The brutal compliment is a splendid
weapon but it requires an education
of the voice, an eloquence of the glance
a dramatic mixture of audacity and
confusion. It had best be let
alone.
